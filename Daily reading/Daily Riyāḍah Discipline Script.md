## 🧠 Daily Riyāḍah Discipline Script

I do not wait to feel motivated before I act.  
Motivation comes and goes. Discipline stays.

My ego is like a child.  
If I leave it unchecked, it will run wild and control me.  
If I train it, it will follow me.

If I let my ego loose, it corrupts me.  
If I discipline it, it aligns with me.

I do not follow my feelings.  
I train them.

Disciplining myself means doing what I do not feel like doing.  
Acting when I feel resistance.  
Stopping when I feel desire.

I pray when I feel tired.  
I leave sin when I want it.  
I stay silent when I want to react.

This is not weakness.  
This is control.

Every time I go against my ego,  
I am rebuilding myself.

This is construction.  
Slow, difficult, unseen — but real.

The problem is not that I cannot change.  
The problem is that I want results without struggle.

I want a pure heart —  
but purity comes from confrontation, not comfort.

Motivation may start the journey.  
But discipline is what completes it.

So I ask myself today:  
Am I a prisoner of my feelings?  
Or the master of my ego?

If I remain firm when I do not feel like it,  
I have already begun real transformation.
