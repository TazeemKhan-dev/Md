# 🧠 Resistance — Daily Cheat Sheet

## 🔑 Core Definition
Resistance = Avoiding what I already know I should do.

---

## 🔍 Quick Test

1. Do I know the right action?
   ❌ No → Not Resistance (clarity problem)  
   ✅ Yes → go next  

2. Am I avoiding it without a real reason?
   ❌ No → Not Resistance (valid reason)  
   ✅ Yes → ✔️ This is Resistance  

---

## 🚫 Not Resistance

• Real fatigue (rest needed)  
• Lack of clarity (don’t know next step)  
• Genuine constraints (time, health, responsibilities)  

→ These need solutions, not fighting.

---

## ⚔️ What is Resistance

• Delaying known work  
• Choosing distractions over meaningful action  
• “I’ll start later” loop  
• Overthinking instead of starting  

---

## 🧭 Mental Model

Resistance = Avoidance of known meaningful action

---

## 🎯 One-Line Rule

"If I know it and I'm not doing it → it's Resistance → just start."

---

# ⚡ How to Apply

## 1. Catch it in real-time

When you think:
• “I’ll start later”  
• “Just one scroll”  
• “Not in the mood”  

Say:
"This is Resistance."

---

## 2. Remove thinking

Don’t plan, don’t optimize, don’t wait.

→ Just start:
• open the file  
• write 1 line  
• read 1 paragraph  

Action → clarity → motivation

---

## 3. Use the 5-Minute Rule

“I’ll just do 5 minutes.”

→ Most times you continue  
→ Even if not, you still win  

---

## 4. Fix the clarity trap

If stuck:
Next step = smallest possible action

Example:
• Not “build project”  
• But “open project + check last file”  

---

## 5. Separate the problem

When stuck, ask:

• Am I tired? → rest  
• Am I confused? → find next step  
• Am I avoiding? → act immediately  

---

## 6. Make it mechanical

Don’t rely on mood.

Use rules:

“If X time → do Y action”

Examples:
• 8 AM → open Time Audit Engine  
• After lunch → 10 min Arabic  

---

# 🔥 Final Reminder

Resistance is not a feeling to solve.  
It is a signal to act.

The moment I recognize it,  
I move — not think.
